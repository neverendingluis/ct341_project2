Libre Baskerville + Source Sans Pro

A good combination of modern sans-serif typefaces

Libre Baskerville has a a formal look. It is classified as a transitional typeface, intended as a refinement of what are now called old-style typefaces of the period, especially those of his most eminent contemporary.

Source Sans Pro is a simple complimenting font when it comes to putting it togther with serif fonts. The typeface is inspired by the forms of the American Type Founders' gothics by Morris Fuller Benton, such as News Gothic, Lightline Gothic and Franklin Gothic, modified with both a larger x-height and character width and more humanist-influenced italic forms.

Ubuntut + Open Sans

A combination of bold and thin fonts

Ubuntut is an OpenType-based font family, designed to be a modern, humanist-style typeface by London-based type foundry Dalton Maag. The font has been designed primarily for use on screen displays, and its spacing and kerning is optimised for body copy sizes.

Open Sans is a complimenting font that goes well with serifs and sans serifs. It's features wide apertures on many letters and a large x-height (tall lower-case letters), the typeface is highly legible on screen and at small sizes. Because it belongs to the humanist genre of sans-serif typefaces, it has a true italic.

Ultra + Slabo 27px

A combination of an extended font and tech looking font

Ultra an ultra bold slab typeface with nods to wood type styles like Clarendon and Egyptian. Strong and dramatic letterforms for titling, a serious, yet friendly, and easily legible typestyle. Perfect for power headlines and titling for impact. It really creates a long and bold look for titles to almost grab your attention more since we don't see this font used as much.

Slabo 27px was the font I felt paired better with Ultra because it matched a similar thickness. This font is currently of Slabo 27px, and Slabo 13px. Each and every font from the assortment is fine-tuned to be used with the pixel measurement in its name.
